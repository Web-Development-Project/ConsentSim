Consent

What is consent?

Consent is defined as "permission for something to happen or agreement to do something". It is generally referred to with regards to sexual intercourse, but can involve anything from kissing, to touching to sex. It is important to make sure you obtain consent before proceeding with any type of sexual activity.

Who can't give consent?

There are certain types of people who are unable to give consent. These include:

    Minors (people under the age of 16)
    Those who are unconscious, asleep, intoxicated, drugged, or otherwise unable to say 'yes'
    Those who have a psychological or decision-making disability that impacts on their ability to understand what they are consenting to

How does consent work?

The way consent works nowadays is simple. Consent should be:

    Enthusiastic - A person should obtain enthusiastic consent before proceeding with an activity. Remember that lack of resistance is not the same as saying yes
    Ongoing - your partner should be able to change their mind at any time, and you will need to get consent every time the activity is performed
    Respected - above all, you should respect your partner's wishes when they do not give consent, and they should respect yours. If either of you become uncomfortable at any time, you have the right to ask to stop, and the other person must listen.

How to ask for consent?

Asking for consent doesn't have to be boring or a mood killer! There are many ways to get consent, it's all about how you ask. Consider questions such as:

    "Can I"
    "Do you want me to..?"
    "Want to try..?"
    "Can we..?"
    "Are you ready to..?"
    "Do you like this?"
    "Do you want to..?"
    "Is this ok?"
    "How do you feel about?"
    "How are you feeling?"

Of course, consent doesn't necessarily have to be given verbally. Non-verbal ways people may give consent include:

    Head nodding
    Pulling someone closer
    Making direct eye contact
    Actively touching someone or initiating sexual activity

However, when checking to see if you have consent, it is important to make sure you go slowly, and stop if the person feels uncomfortable. Some signs that a person might be uncomfortable and want you to stop:

    "No"
    "Stop"
    "Maybe"
    "I'm not sure"
    "I don't want to"
    "I don't think I'm ready"
    "Can we slow things down"
    Pulling away or resisting
    Being silent or not responding
    Agreeing to go on a date
    Showing interest in you

Remember that just because a person says they like you or shows interest in you, it does not mean you have their consent, as consent must be ongoing.

There is a big difference in maturity between a 19-year-old and a 13-year-old, which causes Annie to hold a position of power over Nathan. The situation would be the same if the genders were switched.

This situation is not legally consensual, however, if the situation were to be investigated, it would be unlikely to lead to problems as it is an ethically consenting situation. There is also no age difference between Sophie and Sienna, which indicated that there are no power dynamics.
