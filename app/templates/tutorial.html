What is consent?

Consent is defined as "permission forsomething to happen or agreement to do something".

Who can't give consent?

There are certain types of people who are unable to give consent. These include:

    Minors (people under the age of 18)
    Those who are unconscious, asleep, intoxicated, drugged, or otherwise unable to say 'yes'
    Those who have a psychological or decision-making disability that impacts on their ability to understand what they are consenting to

How does consent work?

The way consent works nowadays is simple. Consent should be:

    Enthusiastic - A person should obtain enthusiastic consent before proceeding with an activity. Remember that lack of resistance is not the same as saying yes
      Ongoing - your partner should be able to change their mind at any time, and you will need to get consent every time the activity is performed

How to ask for consent?

Asking for consent doesn't have to be boring or a mood killer! There are many ways to get consent, it's all about the way you ask. Consider questions such as:

    "Can I"
    "Do you want me to..?"
    "Want to try..?"
    "Can we..?"
    "Are you ready to..?"
    "Do you like this?"
    "Do you want to..?"
    "Is this ok?"
    "How do you feel about?"
    "How are you feeling?"

Some scenarios

  • Nathan and Annie go on a date. John is 13 years old while Annie is 19. After the date, they kiss, and Annie asks Nathan if he wants to have sex with her. Nathan says yes because he really likes Annie, and he feels like he is ready. This situation is not consensual, as one of the participants is under the legal age of consent. There is a big difference in maturity between a 19-year-old and a 13-year-old, which causes Annie to hold a position of power over Nathan. The situation would be the same if the genders were switched.
  • Sophie and Sienna have been dating for a year now. They were childhood best friends, and they know each other very well and care for one another deeply. They are both 15 years old. Recently, they have talked about having sex together. Both are excited, and they talk about safety and their boundaries before proceeding. This situation is not legally consensual, however, if the situation were to be investigated, it would be unlikely to lead to problems as it is an ethically consenting situation. There is also no age difference between Sophie and Sienna, which indicated that there are no power dynamics.
